## 10. Numerical Summary & Highlights for Organisers

### 💡 High-Level Findings for Cycling Nerds:

- **Victoria's Popularity Dominance:** Victoria represents the absolute hotbed of Audax participation, capturing **46.6% of national registrations** (847 out of 1,816) and averaging **12.8 riders per event**—nearly double the national average of 7.1.
- **Queensland's Calendar Volume:** QLD hosts the highest number of active events (89), leading to a strong total registration volume of 626 and a healthy average of 7.0 riders per event.
- **South Australia's Balanced Performance:** SA sits in a solid middle position, averaging **5.1 riders per event** over its 14 active listings, placing it ahead of TAS (3.8), ACT (3.5), NSW (3.5), and WA (1.2).
- **SA's Official Climbing Terrain Density:** Under the official RideWithGPS terrain classification, South Australia leads the nation with **57.8%** of its routes classified as `Climbing` (37 out of 64), significantly exceeding the national average of **31.9%** (274 out of 858). NSW follows closely at **50.6%** (89 out of 176).
- **Victoria and Queensland's Gravel/Unsealed Dominance:** Victoria and Queensland are the premier destinations for unsealed and gravel randonneur routes. Unsealed/unpaved surfaces (Mixed Surfaces, Mostly Unpaved, or Unpaved) make up **33.7%** of Queensland's routes and **33.1%** of Victoria's routes, far exceeding the national average of **22.0%**. In contrast, South Australia's mapped routes are overwhelmingly sealed/paved (**96.9%**), with unsealed segments accounting for just **3.1%** of routes, while Tasmania and Western Australia feature 100% paved routes in the database.
- **Official Route Rating Profiles:** Easy and Intermediate routes dominate the national landscape, accounting for **27.3%** and **54.0%** respectively (over 81.3% combined). South Australia is exceptionally beginner-friendly with **7.8%** Easy and **84.4%** Intermediate routes, and only **7.8%** classified as Difficult (with 0% Very Difficult). Nationally, **18.4%** of routes are classified as Difficult or Very Difficult, with ACT having the highest proportion of Very Difficult routes at **8.3%** (7 out of 84).
- **SA's Average Gradient:** SA routes have a mean average gradient of **1.17%** (Median: **1.19%**). This is higher than NSW (1.11%), VIC (0.99%), QLD (0.98%), TAS (0.84%), WA (0.82%), and ACT (0.74%).
- **WA's Rolling Pack Nature:** Western Australia has a low average gradient (0.82%) and the lowest standard deviation (0.29%). This indicates WA's routes are extremely consistent rolling courses with very few sudden steep ramps.
- **ACT Flat vs. Climbs:** ACT contains the highest percentage of Flat routes (38.1%), but also features 4 Hilly routes. Its standard deviation is high (0.35%), showing a stark division between flat lake cruises and steep mountain passes.
- **Victoria's Scale:** Victoria is the largest state by route count (263 mapped routes) but has a very high proportion of flat/rolling courses (56.6%), proving their calendar values accessibility.

## 11. National Randonneur Super-Averages & Comparisons

This section aggregates the collective achievements and physical dimensions of all 858 routes registered in the national database:

### 🌍 Global Scale Achievements

- **Total Combined Distance:** **190,020.1 km**. This is equivalent to riding around the Earth's equator approximately **4.74 times**, or completing the distance to the Moon and back nearly halfway (**49.4%** of the lunar distance).
- **Total Combined Climbing Elevation:** **1,700,874 metres** of vertical elevation gain. This is equivalent to climbing **Mount Everest 192.2 times** from sea level, or ascending into the Earth's stratosphere (over **170 times** the cruising altitude of a commercial jet).
- **Total Combined Participation (Jan-Jun 2026):** **1,816 rider registrations** across 254 active events, generating an average of **7.1 riders** per organized calendar ride.

### 📈 National Average Route Benchmarks

- **Average Route Length:** **221.5 km** (with a median of **157.1 km**).
- **Average Elevation Gain per Route:** **1,982 m** (with a median of **1,584 m**).
- **National Average Gradient:** **0.99%** (with a median of **0.91%**). This places the national average overall terrain profile into the `🔸 Moderate` category.
- **Moderate + Hilly Dominance:** **51.3%** of all mapped routes nationally (440 out of 858) feature an average gradient above 0.90%, showing that randonneurs across Australia face a high proportion of moderate and steep rolling terrain.
- **Official Terrain & Surface Breakdown:** Nationally, **51.9%** of routes are classified as `Rolling`, **31.9%** as `Climbing`, and **15.9%** as `Flat`. In terms of surfaces, **77.7%** of routes are fully or mostly paved, while **22.0%** feature mixed or unpaved/gravel surfaces, reflecting a growing national trend toward all-road and gravel randonneuring.
- **BA vs. BRM Distribution:** Populaires and local loop designs (`BA`) represent **62.6%** (537 routes), while sanctioned long-distance brevets (`BRM` of 200km+) make up **37.4%** (321 routes).
