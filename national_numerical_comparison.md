## 7. Numerical Summary & Highlights for Organisers

### 💡 High-Level Findings for Cycling Nerds:

- **SA's Climbing Density:** South Australia has **82.8%** of its routes classified as Moderate or Hilly, the highest proportion in Australia. NSW follows closely at 72.2%.
- **SA's Average Gradient:** SA routes have a mean average gradient of **1.17%** (Median: **1.19%**). This is higher than NSW (1.11%), VIC (0.99%), QLD (0.98%), TAS (0.84%), WA (0.82%), and ACT (0.74%).
- **WA's Rolling Pack Nature:** Western Australia has a low average gradient (0.82%) and the lowest standard deviation (0.29%). This indicates WA's routes are extremely consistent rolling courses with very few sudden steep ramps.
- **ACT Flat vs. Climbs:** ACT contains the highest percentage of Flat routes (38.1%), but also features 4 Hilly routes. Its standard deviation is high (0.35%), showing a stark division between flat lake cruises and steep mountain passes.
- **Victoria's Scale:** Victoria is the largest state by route count (263 mapped routes) but has a very high proportion of flat/rolling courses (56.6%), proving their calendar values accessibility.

## 8. National Randonneur Super-Averages & Comparisons

This section aggregates the collective achievements and physical dimensions of all 858 routes registered in the national database:

### 🌍 Global Scale Achievements

- **Total Combined Distance:** **190,020.1 km**. This is equivalent to riding around the Earth's equator approximately **4.74 times**, or completing the distance to the Moon and back nearly halfway (**49.4%** of the lunar distance).
- **Total Combined Climbing Elevation:** **1,700,874 metres** of vertical elevation gain. This is equivalent to climbing **Mount Everest 192.2 times** from sea level, or ascending into the Earth's stratosphere (over **170 times** the cruising altitude of a commercial jet).

### 📈 National Average Route Benchmarks

- **Average Route Length:** **221.5 km** (with a median of **157.1 km**).
- **Average Elevation Gain per Route:** **1,982 m** (with a median of **1,584 m**).
- **National Average Gradient:** **0.99%** (with a median of **0.91%**). This places the national average overall terrain profile into the `🔸 Moderate` category.
- **Moderate + Hilly Dominance:** **51.3%** of all mapped routes nationally (440 out of 858) feature an average gradient above 0.90%, showing that randonneurs across Australia face a high proportion of moderate and steep rolling terrain.
- **BA vs. BRM Distribution:** Populaires and local loop designs (`BA`) represent **62.6%** (537 routes), while sanctioned long-distance brevets (`BRM` of 200km+) make up **37.4%** (321 routes).
